About Us

Friends Organization is a unique organization that provides "non-medical first aid" without requiring professional knowledge in roads and homes, 24 hours a day, except for Sabbath and holidays.

The organization offers assistance in various areas, including: tire change, jump-starting vehicles, refueling water / oil / fuel, on highways, and even unlocking locked vehicles using professional equipment without any damage.

In addition, Friends volunteers perform off-road rescues for stuck vehicles, assist in opening locked house doors, and undergo professional training to rescue people from stuck elevators.

Collaborations

The organization collaborates with all emergency services and assists in rescuing babies locked in vehicles, rescuing people from stuck elevators in situations where there is a concern for human life, and assisting the Israel Police in locating missing persons.

Additionally, collaborations exist with many localities throughout the country to provide prompt routine assistance and integrate organization volunteers into unique projects, such as "City Without Violence" or "SAR" to provide immediate emergency response.

How It All Began

The organization was founded in 2006 and is managed by CEO Israel Almasi, alongside Deputy CEO Leizi Stern. Since then, the organization has grown and developed to the point where thousands of people from all over the country turn to them every month, regardless of religion, ethnicity, or gender, because in any case where they are stuck on the roads, Friends will always be there to rescue and assist.

Where We Stand Today

This is made possible thanks to 65,000 volunteers from all walks of civilian life in Israel, spread across the country, from Dan to Eilat, equipped with experience, knowledge, and extensive professional equipment.

Starting with the national call center, operated by over three hundred and fifty volunteer operators who, through an advanced central system, receive calls, assign tasks to relevant teams, and dispatch available responders in the field, to provide the fastest possible assistance to the stranded citizen.

Our Vision

Our vision is to reach a point where there is at least one volunteer in every building, so that no person will be stuck for more than a few minutes anywhere in the world.

This will make Israel a better place.
